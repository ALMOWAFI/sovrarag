REPAIR GUIDELINE
REPAIR-GUIDE-OP-v1.0: Open Defect (OP) Repair — Manual Copper Bridge Method
Revision: 1.0 | Effective Date: 2025-08-01 | Owner: Manufacturing Engineering

1. SCOPE
This guideline covers the manual copper bridge repair method for Open (OP) defects on PCB conductor traces. This is a temporary repair technique that restores electrical continuity.

2. METHOD DESCRIPTION
Manual copper bridge repair involves placing a thin copper wire or ribbon across the open gap in the conductor trace and soldering it to the intact conductor on both sides of the break.

3. PROCEDURE
Step 1: Clean the repair area with IPA.
Step 2: Apply flux to both sides of the open gap.
Step 3: Cut a copper wire (matching conductor width) to span the gap plus 1mm overlap on each side.
Step 4: Position the wire bridge across the gap using ESD tweezers.
Step 5: Solder both ends of the wire to the intact conductor using a fine-tip iron at 330C.
Step 6: Clean excess flux with IPA.
Step 7: Perform continuity test across the repaired conductor.
Step 8: Perform isolation test to adjacent conductors.

4. LIMITATIONS AND WARNINGS

4.1 Symptom vs. Root Cause:
- Manual bridge repair may restore continuity but does not address repeated conductor formation or etching instability.
- This repair fixes the symptom (broken conductor) but not the root cause (why the conductor broke).
- If the same defect recurs after bridge repair, the repair is classified as ineffective.

4.2 Recurrence Monitoring:
- Track all bridge repairs in QMS with repair_id and board serial number.
- If an OP defect recurs on the same board region within 30 days after bridge repair, do not approve another bridge repair.
- Recurrence after repair indicates a process-level issue (etching parameters, chemical bath condition, or material quality).
- Escalate to engineering for root cause analysis.

4.3 Restrictions:
- Maximum 1 bridge repair per conductor path per board.
- Not approved for safety-critical conductor paths without engineering authorization.
- Not approved for high-frequency signal paths (impedance mismatch risk).
