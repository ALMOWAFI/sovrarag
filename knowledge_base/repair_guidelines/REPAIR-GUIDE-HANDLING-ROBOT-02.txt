REPAIR AND MAINTENANCE GUIDE
REPAIR-GUIDE-HANDLING-ROBOT-02: Handling Robot Repair and Calibration Guide
Equipment: HANDLING-ROBOT-02 | Station Area: LINE-A3 AOI Inspection
Revision: 1.0 | Effective Date: 2025-07-15 | Owner: Equipment Engineering

1. SCOPE
This guide covers repair, maintenance, and calibration procedures for HANDLING-ROBOT-02, the automated PCB transfer robot operating near the conductor-sensitive inspection area on LINE-A3.

2. COMMON REPAIR ACTIONS

2.1 Guide Replacement:
- Replace worn handling guides when visual inspection shows groove wear >0.5mm or surface roughness on contact surfaces.
- Guide replacement alone is a partial repair — it addresses the worn contact surface but does not verify that the robot's path programming, pressure settings, or alignment are correct.

2.2 Contact Surface Polishing:
- Polish robot gripper contact surfaces to remove burrs or debris accumulation.
- Polishing reduces the risk of surface-to-board scratching but does not address misalignment.

3. POST-REPAIR CALIBRATION REQUIREMENTS
- After guide replacement, robot path and pressure calibration must be verified before production restart.
- This is a critical requirement — skipping calibration after guide replacement is the most common cause of recurring conductor scratch (CS) defects.

3.1 Calibration Checklist:
Step 1: Verify robot home position using reference markers.
Step 2: Run path verification program — check all waypoints against nominal coordinates.
Step 3: Measure gripper contact pressure using force gauge — compare to specification (typical: 2-5 N).
Step 4: Run 10 test boards through the handling path — inspect for CS or contact marks.
Step 5: If any CS detected on test boards, adjust path offset and/or pressure and re-test.
Step 6: Log calibration results in maintenance system.

4. RECURRENCE AFTER REPAIR
- If conductor scratch defects recur within 7 days of guide replacement + surface polishing, the repair is classified as ineffective.
- Common reasons for repair ineffectiveness:
  - Robot path was not recalibrated after guide replacement (most common).
  - Gripper pressure was not verified — new guides may change the contact geometry.
  - Root cause is robot axis wear (not guide wear) — requires motor/encoder replacement.
- When repair is ineffective: escalate to engineering for full robot mechanical assessment.
