QUALITY ACCEPTANCE CRITERIA
QA-ACCEPT-REV-C: Acceptance Criteria for PCB-X100 Rev-C Material Revision
Effective Date: 2026-03-01 | Owner: Quality Engineering / Supplier Quality

1. SCOPE
This document defines the acceptance criteria for PCB-X100 boards manufactured with the Rev-C material revision, which uses a new substrate supplier (SUP-077) with a different base material formulation.

2. MATERIAL CHANGE SUMMARY
- PCB-X100 changed from Rev-B to Rev-C substrate.
- New supplier SUP-077 provides the base laminate material.
- The Rev-C material may exhibit slightly different visual appearance (texture, color shade) compared to Rev-B.
- Electrical and mechanical properties meet or exceed Rev-B specifications per qualification testing.

3. VISUAL VARIATION ACCEPTANCE

3.1 Expected Variations:
- Slightly different base material texture (smoother or rougher than Rev-B).
- Minor color shade difference in bare substrate areas.
- Different solder mask adhesion appearance at edges (cosmetic only).

3.2 Acceptance Rule:
- Texture variation on Rev-C base material is acceptable if no conductor damage, foreign object, open, or short is detected.
- Visual differences alone are not grounds for rejection during the qualification period.
- If CV (computer vision) inspection returns no defect detection and manual inspection confirms no functional defect, the board should be passed or monitored.

3.3 Rejection Rule:
- If visual variation is accompanied by confirmed conductor damage, delamination, or electrical defect, reject and escalate to supplier quality.
- If confirmed defect rate on Rev-C exceeds Rev-B historical baseline by >50%, pause qualification and review material specification.

4. QUALIFICATION MONITORING
- All Rev-C qualification batches must be tracked separately in QMS.
- Visual variation reports should be logged but do not count as defect events unless confirmed by inspection.
- Minimum qualification volume: 3 production batches with <2% confirmed defect rate.
