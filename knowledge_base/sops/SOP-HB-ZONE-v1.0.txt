STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE
SOP-HB-ZONE-v1.0: Hole Breakout (HB) Defect — Zone-Based Disposition
Revision: 1.0 | Effective Date: 2026-01-01 | Owner: Quality Engineering

1. SCOPE
This SOP covers the identification and zone-based disposition of Hole Breakout (HB) defects detected during AOI inspection on PCB assemblies.

2. DEFINITION
A Hole Breakout (HB) defect occurs when a drilled hole or via extends beyond the boundaries of its associated annular ring or pad, exposing the PCB substrate. HB can affect electrical connectivity and mechanical integrity of plated through-holes.

3. ZONE-BASED SEVERITY
The severity and disposition of HB defects depends on the board zone where the defect is located:

3.1 Functional Interconnect Zone:
- HB in functional interconnect zones (active circuit area, power/signal routing, component mounting pads) is critical.
- May affect electrical connectivity, impedance, or mechanical joint strength.
- Requires manual review or rejection depending on severity.

3.2 Test Coupon Area:
- Hole breakout in test coupon area should be logged for process monitoring but does not automatically reject the product.
- Test coupons are used for process monitoring and qualification — they are not part of the functional circuit.
- HB in test coupons indicates a potential process drift (drill alignment, registration accuracy) that should be monitored.
- The product itself is unaffected if functional circuit areas show no HB.

4. DISPOSITION RULES

4.1 Functional Zone HB:
- Confirmed HB in functional zone: manual_review or reject depending on breakout extent.
- If breakout exceeds 90 degrees of annular ring circumference: reject.
- If breakout is less than 90 degrees and copper connection is maintained: manual_review.

4.2 Test Coupon Zone HB:
- Log the defect for process monitoring records.
- Do not automatically reject the product board.
- Notify process engineering if test coupon HB rate exceeds 5% per batch.
- Recommended disposition: log_and_monitor.

4.3 Zone Determination:
- Use board zone mapping (if available) to determine whether detected HB coordinates fall in functional or test coupon areas.
- If zone mapping is not available, default to manual_review.

5. ESCALATION
- Functional zone HB, severe: reject.
- Functional zone HB, minor: manual_review.
- Test coupon zone HB: log_and_monitor.
- Test coupon HB rate >5% per batch: escalate to process engineering for drill alignment review.
