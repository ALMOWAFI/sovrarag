STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE
SOP-BMFO-v1.2: Base Material Foreign Object (BMFO) Defect Handling
Revision: 1.2 | Effective Date: 2025-11-01 | Owner: Quality Engineering

1. SCOPE
This SOP covers the identification, disposition, and escalation of Base Material Foreign Object (BMFO) defects detected during AOI inspection on PCB assemblies.

2. DEFINITION
BMFO refers to foreign particles, fibers, dust, or debris found on the base material (substrate) of a PCB. These objects are located on the non-conductive laminate surface and are distinguished from Conductor Foreign Object (CFO) defects, which involve objects on or near conductor paths.

3. DETECTION
BMFO defects are typically detected by AOI systems as anomalous regions on the base material surface. Detection confidence depends on object size, contrast with substrate, and lighting conditions.

4. DISPOSITION RULES

4.1 Single Occurrence (No Recurrence Pattern):
- If the foreign object is only on base material and not touching conductor paths, vias, or coating-sensitive areas: clean the affected board and reinspect.
- If the object cannot be removed by standard cleaning, route to manual review.
- Do NOT automatically reject unless the object affects conductors or sensitive areas.

4.2 Recurring at Same Station:
- If BMFO defects are recurring at the same station, check the contamination source.
- Review maintenance logs for dust accumulation, environmental controls, and handling equipment.
- If temporary cleaning has not resolved the issue, escalate to full station cleaning per CLEANING-PROCEDURE-AOI-v2.0.
- Recurring BMFO at the same station with the same product suggests a station-level contamination problem, not a board-level issue.

4.3 Recurring Across Lines or Factories:
- If BMFO defects are found across multiple lines or factories with the same supplier lot, investigate supplier material quality.

5. ESCALATION
- Single board BMFO with no conductor involvement: clean_and_reinspect.
- Recurring BMFO at same station (3+ cases in 30 days): clean_station and implement contamination monitoring.
- BMFO touching conductor paths: reclassify as CFO and follow SOP-CONTAMINATION-v1.1.
