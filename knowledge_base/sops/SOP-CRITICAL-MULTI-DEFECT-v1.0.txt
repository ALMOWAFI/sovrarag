STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE
SOP-CRITICAL-MULTI-DEFECT-v1.0: Multiple Critical Electrical Defect Handling
Revision: 1.0 | Effective Date: 2026-01-15 | Owner: Quality Engineering

1. SCOPE
This SOP covers the disposition of PCB assemblies where multiple critical electrical defects (such as Open and Short) are detected on the same board during AOI or electrical inspection.

2. DEFINITION
A multi-critical defect condition exists when a single board has two or more confirmed or suspected critical electrical defects. Critical electrical defects include:
- Short (SH): Unintended conductor connection
- Open (OP): Conductor discontinuity
- Missing safety component
- VCC-GND bridge

3. SEVERITY
Multi-critical defect boards represent severe reliability risk. The combination of Open and Short defects on the same board indicates potential systemic process failure affecting conductor formation, etching, or lamination.

4. DISPOSITION RULES

4.1 Mandatory Rule:
- If a board has multiple critical electrical defects such as Open and Short, automatic pass is not allowed.
- Board must be rejected or sent to engineering-controlled rework.
- Standard rework procedures do not apply — engineering must evaluate whether rework is feasible and safe.

4.2 Engineering-Controlled Rework:
- Only permitted if engineering confirms that both defects can be independently verified and repaired without introducing new risks.
- Post-rework verification must include full electrical testing (continuity, isolation, functional test).
- If engineering rework fails, the board must be rejected — no second rework attempt.

4.3 Batch-Level Investigation:
- If multi-critical defects appear in the same batch family (2+ cases in 14 days), investigate batch-level process parameters.
- Review previous disposition history — if historical outcomes show high rejection or rework failure rates, tighten disposition to immediate rejection.
- No standard repair guideline should override the multi-critical defect SOP.

5. ESCALATION
- Single board with multi-critical defects: reject or escalate_engineering.
- Multi-critical pattern in batch family: quarantine_batch + escalate_engineering.
- Previous engineering rework failed on same batch: reject.

6. CONFIDENCE ASSESSMENT
- If CV confidence for both defects is high (>0.70): high confidence in multi-critical classification.
- If CV confidence is medium (0.50-0.70): medium confidence — still reject or escalate, but note uncertainty.
- If CV confidence is low (<0.50): manual verification required before multi-critical classification.
