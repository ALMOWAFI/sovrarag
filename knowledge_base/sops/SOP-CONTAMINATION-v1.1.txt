STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE
SOP-CONTAMINATION-v1.1: Foreign Object Contamination Near Conductor Regions
Revision: 1.1 | Effective Date: 2025-10-15 | Owner: Quality Engineering

1. SCOPE
This SOP covers the identification, disposition, and escalation of Conductor Foreign Object (CFO) defects — foreign material detected on or near conductor paths during AOI inspection.

2. DEFINITION
A Conductor Foreign Object (CFO) is a foreign particle, fiber, debris, or contaminant found on or in close proximity to conductor paths, pads, vias, or other electrically active areas of a PCB. CFO is more serious than BMFO (Base Material Foreign Object) because foreign objects on conductor regions may interfere with electrical behavior, cause shorts, or degrade reliability.

3. DISTINCTION FROM BMFO
- BMFO: Foreign object on base material (substrate), away from conductors. Lower risk.
- CFO: Foreign object on or near conductor regions. Higher risk — may cause electrical interference.
- When in doubt about whether the object is on base material or conductor, classify as CFO.

4. DISPOSITION RULES

4.1 Single Occurrence:
- Foreign object defects near conductor regions require manual review and station contamination check.
- If the object can be safely removed without conductor damage, clean and reinspect.
- If the object is embedded or cannot be removed cleanly, reject the board.

4.2 Shift-Correlated Pattern:
- If CFO detections concentrate on a specific shift (e.g., night shift), investigate shift-specific environmental factors: HVAC operation, cleaning schedules, operator handling practices.
- Correlate with maintenance logs for dust accumulation or environmental control issues.

4.3 Station Contamination Pattern:
- If CFO and/or BMFO detections are recurring at the same station, this indicates a station-level contamination source.
- Temporary cleaning is not sufficient if foreign object detections continue. Full station cleaning is required per CLEANING-PROCEDURE-AOI-v2.0.
- After full cleaning, monitor for 3 shifts to confirm resolution.

5. ESCALATION
- Single CFO, removable: clean_and_reinspect.
- Recurring CFO at same station: clean_station.
- CFO pattern linked to specific shift: clean_station + shift-specific investigation.
- CFO with confirmed electrical impact: reject.
