TECHNICAL MANUAL
TECH-PCB-X100-VISUAL-SENSITIVITY: Visual Inspection Sensitivity for Reflective Copper Regions
Product: PCB-X100 | Applicable Revisions: Rev-B, Rev-C

1. PURPOSE
This document describes the visual inspection challenges related to reflective copper trace regions on PCB-X100 and provides guidance for interpreting AOI results on boards with high-reflectivity surfaces.

2. REFLECTIVE COPPER CHARACTERISTICS
- PCB-X100 uses a standard copper trace finish that can be highly reflective after etching.
- Reflective copper trace regions can be sensitive to lighting profile changes during AOI inspection.
- The degree of reflectivity varies with:
  - Copper thickness and surface roughness
  - Etching chemistry and process parameters
  - Time since etching (oxidation reduces reflectivity over hours)
  - Ambient humidity

3. AOI INTERACTION WITH REFLECTIVE SURFACES

3.1 Normal Operation:
- AOI systems compensate for copper reflectivity using multi-angle lighting.
- Reference thresholds are calibrated to account for expected reflectivity range.

3.2 After Calibration Events:
- Changes to lighting angles or intensity during calibration can alter how reflective surfaces appear to the AOI camera.
- Copper trace reflection can increase false positives for copper-residue-like defects (such as Spurious Copper, SC) after lighting changes.
- Spurious Copper detections may increase by 20-50% after lighting recalibration on boards with high reflective copper regions.

3.3 Distinguishing Real SC from Reflection Artifacts:
- Real Spurious Copper: Extra copper material physically present, visible under multiple lighting angles.
- Reflection Artifact: Appears only under specific lighting angles, disappears when board is tilted.
- Manual verification is the definitive method to distinguish real SC from reflection.

4. RECOMMENDATIONS
- After AOI calibration, monitor SC detection rates on PCB-X100 for 5 shifts.
- If SC detection rate increases >20% and manual confirmation rate drops below 60%, suspect lighting-induced false positives.
- Cross-reference with SOP-AOI-CAL-v1.4 for calibration drift monitoring procedure.
