TECHNICAL MANUAL
TECH-PCB-X100-CONTAMINATION-RISK: Foreign Object Contamination Risk on Conductor Regions
Product: PCB-X100 | Applicable Revisions: Rev-B, Rev-C

1. PURPOSE
This document describes the risk and impact of foreign object contamination specifically on conductor regions of PCB-X100, distinguishing between base material contamination (BMFO) and conductor contamination (CFO).

2. CONTAMINATION RISK CLASSIFICATION

2.1 Base Material Foreign Object (BMFO):
- Foreign object located on the non-conductive substrate surface.
- Risk: LOW to MEDIUM depending on proximity to conductors.
- Impact: Generally cosmetic unless object migrates to conductor during subsequent processing.
- Disposition: Clean and reinspect (single occurrence), station cleaning (recurring).

2.2 Conductor Foreign Object (CFO):
- Foreign object located on or near conductor traces, pads, or vias.
- Risk: MEDIUM to HIGH.
- Foreign objects on conductor regions are more serious than foreign objects on base material because they may interfere with electrical behavior.
- Impact: Potential short circuit, impedance change, or solder joint interference.
- Disposition: Manual review required. Clean only if object can be removed without conductor damage.

3. CONTAMINATION SOURCES
- Dust and particulate from production environment (especially near board loading areas).
- Debris from handling equipment (robot grippers, guide rails, conveyor belts).
- Fiber contamination from operator clothing or cleaning materials.
- Solder balls or flux residue from upstream reflow process.

4. SHIFT-CORRELATED CONTAMINATION
- Contamination patterns often correlate with specific shifts due to:
  - Different HVAC operating modes (day vs. night)
  - Different cleaning schedules
  - Different operator handling practices
- Night shift contamination is commonly higher due to reduced environmental monitoring.

5. STATION-LEVEL CONTAMINATION
- If both BMFO and CFO detections concentrate at the same station, this indicates a station-level contamination source.
- Station-level contamination requires full cleaning per CLEANING-PROCEDURE-AOI-v2.0.
- Temporary cleaning is insufficient for station-level contamination.
