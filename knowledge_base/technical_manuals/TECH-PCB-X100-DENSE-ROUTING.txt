TECHNICAL MANUAL
TECH-PCB-X100-DENSE-ROUTING: Dense Conductor Routing Risk Assessment
Product: PCB-X100 | Applicable Revisions: Rev-B, Rev-C

1. PURPOSE
This document assesses the electrical reliability risk associated with dense conductor routing areas on PCB-X100, particularly in the context of Short (SH) defect evaluation.

2. DENSE ROUTING CHARACTERISTICS
- PCB-X100 has dense conductor routing in the BCM-01 power distribution and signal processing regions.
- Minimum trace spacing: 0.12mm in the densest areas.
- Minimum trace width: 0.15mm.
- Number of conductor layers in dense region: 4 (all layers active).

3. RISK ASSESSMENT

3.1 Short Defect Risk:
- Dense conductor routing increases electrical reliability risk when short defects occur.
- Shorts in dense areas are more likely to affect multiple signal paths simultaneously.
- Dense routing makes rework more difficult — adjacent traces are easily damaged during repair.
- Risk of undetected secondary shorts increases with routing density.

3.2 Process Sensitivity:
- Dense routing areas are more sensitive to:
  - Etching process variation (under-etch leaves copper bridges)
  - Incoming material quality (laminate surface cleanliness)
  - Registration accuracy (layer-to-layer alignment)

4. INSPECTION REQUIREMENTS
- Dense routing areas require higher AOI resolution (minimum 15 um/pixel).
- X-ray inspection may be required for inner-layer shorts in 4-layer regions.
- Electrical testing (flying probe or ICT) is recommended for all dense-routing boards.

5. DEFECT DISPOSITION IN DENSE AREAS
- SH in dense routing: Higher severity than SH in standard routing.
- If SH is linked to supplier material lot, dense routing increases the urgency for supplier containment.
- Rework of shorts in dense routing is not recommended — rejection is preferred unless engineering approves specific procedure.
