TECHNICAL MANUAL
TECH-PCB-X100-HANDLING-RISK: Handling Risk Assessment for Fine Conductor Traces
Product: PCB-X100 | Applicable Revisions: Rev-B, Rev-C

1. PURPOSE
This document assesses the risk of mechanical damage to PCB-X100 conductor traces during automated and manual handling operations, particularly near the handling robot path.

2. CONDUCTOR VULNERABILITY

2.1 Fine Trace Areas:
- Fine conductor traces near the handling path are sensitive to mechanical contact and misalignment.
- PCB-X100 has conductor traces as narrow as 0.15mm in the BCM-01 region.
- These traces are located near the board edges where handling robot grippers and guide rails make contact.

2.2 Risk Factors:
- Robot gripper misalignment: Even 0.5mm offset can cause gripper contact with conductor areas.
- Excessive contact pressure: Worn or improperly adjusted grippers apply uneven pressure.
- Guide rail wear: Worn rails create vibration and lateral board movement during transport.
- Conveyor speed: Excessive speed increases the chance of board-to-fixture contact.

3. HANDLING-RELATED DEFECT TYPES
- CS (Conductor Scratch): Most common. Caused by direct mechanical contact with conductor surface.
- CFO (Conductor Foreign Object): Secondary. Caused by debris from worn handling components deposited on the board.
- Pad damage: Rare. Caused by excessive gripper pressure on pad areas.

4. POST-REPAIR VERIFICATION
- After any handling robot repair (guide replacement, gripper adjustment, conveyor modification):
  - Robot path and pressure calibration must be verified before production restart.
  - Guide replacement alone does not verify that the robot path and pressure are correct.
  - If conductor scratch defects recur after repair, the repair is classified as ineffective.
  - Full recalibration of robot path, pressure, and alignment is required.

5. MONITORING
- Track CS defect rate per station per shift after any handling equipment changes.
- If CS rate exceeds 2% per shift after repair, halt production and escalate to equipment engineering.
